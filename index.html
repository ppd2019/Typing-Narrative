Typing terminologies

Basically, typing is the act of writing or inputting text by pressing the designated keys on a typewriter, computer keyboard, cell phone, calculator, or any other device which lets you input data through letters, numbers or symbols.
There are three methods of measuring one’s typing speed.

  • Characters per Minute (CPM)

    Any key which prints on your screen is counted as a character. Number of characters typed in a minute would be considered your CPM speed.

  • Words per Minute (WPM)

    Number of correct words typed in a minute is the data used to measure your WPM speed.

  • Key Strokes per Hour/ Key Depressions per Hour (DPH)

    Key strokes/ key depressions have the same definition as characters specified above. However, this method measures the numbers of key depressions you are able to make in an hour instead of a minute.

History of Typing

Typing is omnipresent, but it has developed exponentially to reach its current popularity as a method of data input. The first typing devices were designed and patented in the 1700s while the first manufactured typing devices came about in the 1870s. Since then, technology and design has come a long way to improve the efficiency of the activity. The QWERTY layout of keys, however, has remained the same. Established with the advent of typewriters, computers, and smartphones have made typing an everyday activity now.
From its humble beginnings in the form of a typewriter to the technological advances which led to touch screen typing in the 21st century, typing has advanced significantly.

All of this brings us to the question, why do we even need to type fast?
Learning to type fast and accurately will help you in many ways in life, and it should be considered an essential skill for anyone who sees themselves working with a computer in some capacity. Some reasons why earning how to type fast is beneficial in life and work are listed below.

  • Typing faster and accurately will help you save time. Small amounts of time saved by faster typing skills add up to a considerable amount.
  • Typing faster increases productivity. If you learn to double the speed you type, you can essentially get twice as much done in the same space of time.
  • Faster typing speed will mean that you will be able to reduce the time spent at your desk, and that will lead to more time spent standing up and stretching so as not to hurt your back, neck, and shoulders.
  • Typing faster means that you will be able to improve your focus. You lose concentration every time you look down and try to find a key. Knowing how to type faster will help you focus more on what you are trying to say than the actual letters that you are typing.

All of this sounds valid. But how will we be able to achieve this?
Here are some useful tips to help you type faster.

  1. Improve Your Workspace : Your workspace should be clean, properly ventilated, and comfortable. Also, for optimal typing, you will need to get a table and not work with your laptop or computer on your lap.
  2. Fix Your Posture : If you want to type well, the correct posture will be seated, straight backed, and with your feet planted a little apart, flat on the ground. You wrists should also be positioned in such a way that your fingers can cover the keyboard. Tilt your head a bit as you can look at the screen properly as well. It is also very important that you keep this position as you type.
  3. Familiarize Yourself with the Keyboard : Work on memorizing the positions of the letter keys, as well as some of the most used punctuation marks. You will need to understand where they are without looking at the keyboard. One way to remember the keys is to close your eyes and say the keys out loud as you press them.
  4. Slowly With Touch-Typing : The quickest way to master typing will be learning touch typing. Once you can type key combinations without looking at the keyboard layout, your speed will increase.
  5. Don’t Look at Your Hands : Keep yourself from looking at your keyboard as you type so that your fingers are made to learn how the keys work.
  6. Practice, Practice, Practice : Mastering the touch typing technique will prove to be a bit finicky, but once your posture is up and you get your fingers where they should be, you can only improve by practicing.

Typing fast can come in handy even when you are applying for jobs. Even as more and more of us are talking into our phones and computers these days, typing is still certain to play an important role in our lives for many years to come.
Learning how to type well will never be a waste of time.
